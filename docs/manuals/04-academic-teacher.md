# Shule SMS — User Manual
## Role: Academic Teacher

---

## 1. Introduction

The **Academic Teacher** role is responsible for academic coordination across the school. You oversee attendance, manage and monitor exams for all levels, supervise staff, and send communications to parents. You have broader access than a regular class teacher but do not manage finances.

---

## 2. Getting Started

### Logging In
1. Open the Shule SMS URL.
2. Enter your **email** and **password**. Click **Sign In**.
3. You land on the **Dashboard**.

### Your Sidebar
| Menu Item | What it does |
|---|---|
| Dashboard | School overview and key metrics |
| Students | View student records and profiles |
| Attendance | Mark and view attendance across all classes |
| Exams | Create exams, enter marks, view results |
| Staff | View staff profiles, class assignments, leave |
| Communications | Send messages to parents |

---

## 3. Dashboard

Your dashboard shows:
- **Total Students**, **Fees Collected** (read-only view), **Outstanding Fees**, **Today's Attendance**
- **Monthly Revenue chart** — fee collection trends
- **Top Defaulters** — you can send a fee reminder via WhatsApp from here

---

## 4. Students

You can view all student records but cannot enrol new students.

1. Click **Students** in the sidebar.
2. Search by name, ID, or phone. Filter by level or status.
3. Click any student to view their profile:
   - **Overview** — personal details
   - **Attendance** — their monthly attendance record
   - **Exams** — their results and report card

---

## 5. Attendance

### Marking Attendance
1. Click **Attendance**.
2. Click **Mark Attendance**.
3. Select: **Date**, **Session** (Morning/Afternoon), **Level**, and **Stream**.
4. Mark each student: **Present**, **Absent**, **Late**, or **Excused**.
5. Click **Submit**. Records can be corrected if made in error.

> As Academic Teacher, you can mark attendance for any class. Class teachers are restricted to their own class.

### Viewing Attendance Records
Use the date, level, stream, month, and status filters to review records. The **Summary** card at the top shows today's school-wide attendance rate.

### Absentees
Filter by today's date to see all absent students with guardian contact information — useful for coordinating absence follow-ups with class teachers.

---

## 6. Exams

### Creating an Exam
1. Click **Exams → + Create Exam**.
2. Fill in:
   - **Title**, **Subject**, **Exam Type** (CA1, CA2, Mid-Term, Terminal, Mock)
   - **Level**, **Term** (Term 1 or Term 2), **Quarter** (Q1–Q4)
   - **Date** and **Maximum Score**
3. Click **Create**. Subject teachers for this exam's subject are notified automatically.

### Entering Marks
1. Click the **pen icon** on an exam row.
2. Enter marks for each student. Grades (A–F) are auto-calculated.
3. For classes you are directly responsible for, enter marks yourself.
4. For other classes, supervise that subject/class teachers enter their marks.
5. Click **Save All Marks**.

> As Academic Teacher, you can enter marks for any exam. Subject Teachers are restricted to their assigned subjects; Class Teachers to their own class.

### Viewing Results
Click the **chart icon** on any exam to see the full results page — marks, grades, class average, highest and lowest scores. Use **View Report Card** for individual student reports.

---

## 7. Staff

You can view all staff profiles but cannot add or remove staff members — that is done by the Headteacher.

1. Click **Staff** in the sidebar.
2. Browse or search for staff members.
3. View their designation, subjects taught, and contact details.

**Class Assignments** — you can view which teacher is assigned to each class. Assignments are managed by the Headteacher.

**Leave Requests** — you can view leave requests but approval is handled by the Headteacher.

---

## 8. Communications

### Sending a Message to Parents
1. Click **Communications**.
2. Click **+ New Message**.
3. Choose:
   - **Channel**: WhatsApp or Email
   - **Audience**: All Students, By Level, By Class, or Individual
   - Enter **Subject** and **Message**.
4. Click **Send**.

**Use cases:**
- Announce exam timetables to parents of a specific level
- Notify all parents about a school event
- Follow up individually on a student's performance

---

## 9. Common Workflows

**Before exams:**
1. Create all exam records in the **Exams** section.
2. Confirm subject teachers know which exams they are responsible for.
3. The system auto-notifies assigned teachers when an exam is created.

**After exams:**
1. Check that all marks have been entered (Exams list shows which are complete).
2. Review the class averages in the Results view.
3. Flag any exams with unusually low averages to the Headteacher.

**Weekly attendance review:**
1. Go to **Attendance**, set **Month** filter to the current month.
2. Review per-student attendance percentages in the Summary view.
3. Identify students below 80% attendance and coordinate with class teachers.
