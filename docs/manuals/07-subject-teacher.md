# Shule SMS — User Manual
## Role: Subject Teacher

---

## 1. Introduction

As a **Subject Teacher**, your primary responsibility in Shule SMS is **entering marks** for the subjects you teach. You can view student records and exam results, but you cannot mark attendance or manage fees. Your access is focused on academic results.

---

## 2. Getting Started

### Logging In
1. Open the Shule SMS URL in your browser.
2. Enter your **email** and **password**. Click **Sign In**.
3. You land on the **Dashboard**.

### Your Sidebar
| Menu Item | What it does |
|---|---|
| Dashboard | School overview |
| Students | View student records and exam results |
| Exams | Enter marks for your subjects |

---

## 3. Dashboard

The Dashboard shows school-wide statistics. For your role it is mainly informational — total students, attendance rate, and fee summaries (read-only).

---

## 4. Students

### Finding a Student
1. Click **Students** in the sidebar.
2. Search by name, student ID, or phone number.
3. Filter by **Level** to narrow results.
4. Click a student's row to open their profile.

### Student Profile
As a Subject Teacher you can view:
- **Overview** — personal details and guardian contacts
- **Attendance** — their attendance history (read-only)
- **Exams** — all their exam results across subjects

> You cannot modify student details or attendance records.

---

## 5. Exams

Exams are created by the Academic Teacher or Headteacher. When an exam is created for a subject you teach, you will receive a notification (in-app or email depending on system configuration).

### Finding Exams for Your Subjects
1. Click **Exams** in the sidebar.
2. Use the **Level** and **Quarter** filters to narrow the list.
3. Exams you are responsible for will be listed. You can only enter marks for exams in your assigned subjects.

### Entering Marks
1. Find the exam in the list.
2. Click the **pen (marks entry) icon** on the exam row.
3. The student list for that level/exam appears.
4. Enter each student's score in the marks column.
5. The **grade** (A–F) is calculated automatically as you type.
6. Click **Save All Marks**.

> If an exam is not showing or you cannot access its marks entry, confirm with the Academic Teacher that you are assigned to that subject and the correct level.

### Correcting a Mark
1. Go to **Exams**, find the exam.
2. Click the **pen icon** to re-open the marks entry page.
3. Update the score for the student.
4. Click **Save All Marks** again.

> Marks can be corrected at any time before the Academic Teacher locks the exam results.

### Viewing Results
1. Click the **chart icon** on any exam row.
2. The Results page shows all marks, grades, the class average, and the highest and lowest scores.
3. Click **View Report Card** on any student row to see their full cumulative report.

---

## 6. Grading Reference

Marks are entered as raw scores (e.g. 0–100). The system assigns grades automatically:

| Grade | Range (approximate) |
|---|---|
| A | 75% and above |
| B | 60–74% |
| C | 45–59% |
| D | 30–44% |
| F | Below 30% |

> Exact grade boundaries depend on the school configuration. Ask the Academic Teacher if you are unsure.

**O-Level Division (Form 1–4):**
The system calculates the student's aggregate score across subjects and assigns a Division (I–IV) automatically on the report card.

---

## 7. Common Workflows

**After administering an exam:**
1. Mark the scripts manually.
2. Open **Exams** in Shule SMS.
3. Find the exam, click the pen icon.
4. Enter all marks. Click **Save All Marks**.
5. Open the Results page to verify all entries are correct.

**Checking your class's performance:**
1. Go to **Exams**, find the exam.
2. Click the chart icon to open Results.
3. Review the class average and individual grades.
4. If many students scored below 45%, discuss with the Academic Teacher.

**A parent asks about their child's marks:**
1. Go to **Students**, find the student.
2. Open their **Exams** tab.
3. All results across all subjects are visible there.

**You receive a notification about a new exam:**
1. Open the notification (in-app).
2. Go to **Exams**, find the newly created exam.
3. Confirm the details (date, subject, level) are correct.
4. After administering the exam, enter marks within the deadline given by the Academic Teacher.
