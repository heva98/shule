# Shule SMS — User Manual
## Role: Teacher (General)

---

## 1. Introduction

The **Teacher** role is the general-purpose teaching role in Shule SMS. It is a legacy role that gives access to students, attendance, and exams across the school. If you have been assigned a more specific role (Class Teacher or Subject Teacher), refer to those manuals instead — they give more focused guidance.

As a General Teacher you can:
- View all student records
- Mark attendance for any class
- Enter exam marks for any exam
- View all results and report cards

You do not manage fees, staff, or communications.

---

## 2. Getting Started

### Logging In
1. Open the Shule SMS URL in your browser.
2. Enter your **email** and **password**. Click **Sign In**.
3. You land on the **Dashboard**.

### Your Sidebar
| Menu Item | What it does |
|---|---|
| Dashboard | School overview and key metrics |
| Students | View student records |
| Attendance | Mark and view attendance for any class |
| Exams | Enter marks and view results for any exam |

---

## 3. Dashboard

The Dashboard shows school-wide statistics:
- Total Students enrolled
- Fees Collected and Outstanding (read-only)
- Today's Attendance rate

---

## 4. Students

1. Click **Students** in the sidebar.
2. Search by name, student ID, or phone. Filter by level or status.
3. Click a student to view their profile.

**Profile tabs available to you:**
- **Overview** — personal details and guardian contacts
- **Attendance** — monthly attendance history and rate
- **Exams** — all exam results and report card

---

## 5. Attendance

Unlike a Class Teacher (who is restricted to one class), you can mark attendance for any class.

### Marking a Register
1. Click **Attendance → Mark Attendance**.
2. Select: **Date**, **Session** (Morning/Afternoon), **Level**, and **Stream**.
3. Mark each student: **Present**, **Absent**, **Late**, or **Excused**.
4. Click **Submit**.

### Viewing Records
Use the filters (date, level, stream, month) to review historical records. The Summary view shows per-student attendance percentages.

---

## 6. Exams

### Viewing Exams
1. Click **Exams** in the sidebar.
2. Use the Level and Quarter filters to find relevant exams.

### Entering Marks
1. Click the **pen icon** on an exam row.
2. Enter each student's raw score.
3. Grades (A–F) are auto-calculated.
4. Click **Save All Marks**.

### Viewing Results
Click the **chart icon** on any exam to open the Results page. Click **View Report Card** on any student row for their full report.

---

## 7. Common Workflows

**Daily attendance (for your class):**
1. Go to **Attendance → Mark Attendance**.
2. Select your level and stream.
3. Mark and submit before 09:30.

**Post-exam mark entry:**
1. Go to **Exams**, find the exam.
2. Click the pen icon, enter marks, and save.
3. Check the Results page to verify.

**Checking a student's overall performance:**
1. Go to **Students**, find the student.
2. Open the **Exams tab** to see all results across subjects.
3. Open the **Attendance tab** to check their attendance rate.

---

## 8. Note on Role Transition

The **Teacher** role was designed as a general access level. Your school may be transitioning to more specific roles (**Class Teacher** or **Subject Teacher**) that better reflect your responsibilities. If your role is updated by the System Administrator, your access may change. Contact the Headteacher for clarification on your specific responsibilities.
