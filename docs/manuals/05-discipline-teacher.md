# Shule SMS — User Manual
## Role: Discipline Teacher

---

## 1. Introduction

The **Discipline Teacher** is responsible for managing student conduct and disciplinary matters. In Shule SMS, you can view student records, monitor attendance, record disciplinary incidents, and track their resolution. You do not have access to fees, exams, or staff management.

---

## 2. Getting Started

### Logging In
1. Open the Shule SMS URL.
2. Enter your **email** and **password**. Click **Sign In**.
3. You land on the **Dashboard**.

### Your Sidebar
| Menu Item | What it does |
|---|---|
| Dashboard | School overview and key metrics |
| Students | View student records and profiles |
| Attendance | View attendance records across all classes |

> Disciplinary incident management is accessed through the **Staff** section by senior staff. When an incident is referred to you, you will receive an in-app notification (the bell icon, if available).

---

## 3. Dashboard

Your dashboard shows the same school-wide overview as other staff:
- Total Students, Fees (view only), Today's Attendance
- Monthly Revenue chart (view only)
- Top Defaulters list (view only)

---

## 4. Students

### Finding a Student
1. Click **Students** in the sidebar.
2. Search by name, student ID, or phone number.
3. Filter by **Level** or **Status** as needed.
4. Click a student's row to open their profile.

### Student Profile
When you open a student's profile you can see:
- **Overview** — personal details, guardian names and contact numbers (useful for calling home)
- **Attendance** — their attendance history and rate
- **Exams** — their academic performance (read-only for your role)

> Guardian contact information (phone and WhatsApp) is visible on the student's Overview tab. Use this to contact parents when following up on disciplinary matters.

---

## 5. Attendance

You can view attendance records across the whole school but **cannot mark** attendance yourself — that is the responsibility of Class Teachers and the Academic Teacher.

### Viewing Attendance
1. Click **Attendance** in the sidebar.
2. Use the filters to narrow records:
   - **Date** or **Month** — specific day or period
   - **Level** and **Stream** — specific class
   - **Status** — filter for Absent or Late students
3. The **Daily Summary** card shows today's school-wide attendance rate.

### Using Attendance for Discipline
Consistent absenteeism or lateness can be an indicator of conduct issues. To identify patterns:
1. Go to **Attendance**, filter by a student's name (click their record in the Students section and open the Attendance tab on their profile).
2. Review their monthly attendance chart and percentage.
3. If the rate is below 80%, escalate to the Headteacher or contact the guardian.

---

## 6. Notifications

When a Class Teacher or Headteacher records a disciplinary incident and refers it to you, you will receive an **in-app notification**. Look for the notification badge in the top bar (if visible), or check your email/WhatsApp if the system is configured to send notifications.

---

## 7. Common Workflows

**Following up on a referred incident:**
1. You receive a notification that an incident has been referred to you.
2. Open the **Students** section and search for the student involved.
3. Review their profile — check attendance patterns and any notes.
4. Contact the guardian using the phone number on their Overview tab.
5. Report back to the Headteacher with your findings or resolution.
6. The Headteacher or Academic Teacher will mark the incident as Resolved in the system.

**Investigating frequent absenteeism:**
1. Go to **Students**, find the student.
2. Open their **Attendance tab**.
3. Check their monthly rate. Anything below 80% warrants a parent call.
4. Open the **Overview tab** to get the guardian's WhatsApp number.
5. Send or call to confirm the absences are known to the family.

**Checking a class's conduct:**
1. Go to **Attendance**, filter by the class (Level + Stream) and the current month.
2. Look for multiple students with **Late** or **Absent** patterns.
3. Cross-reference with the Class Teacher for context.
